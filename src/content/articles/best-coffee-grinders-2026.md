**Price**: $700 | **Type**: Electric conical burr | **Settings**: Stepless | **Hopper**: Single dose

The Niche Zero solved a problem that the grinder industry spent decades ignoring: retention. Most grinders retain ground coffee inside the mechanism from one use to the next, which goes stale and contaminates subsequent doses. The Niche Zero retains under 0.1g — effectively zero.

The 63mm flat conical burrs (Swiss-made Mazzer-style) produce exceptional grind uniformity across the espresso range. The stepless adjustment (infinite positions rather than click stops) allows dialling in extraction with precision that step-based grinders don't allow.

Single-dose design means you weigh your beans, load them, grind directly into a portafilter or container, and have nothing left over. No stale retention from yesterday's dose. The hopper is optional (included but most users don't install it).

At $700 the Niche Zero is a professional purchase for a home setup. The extraction improvement from a consumer grinder to a Niche Zero on a quality espresso machine is audible in the cup — tighter, cleaner, more consistent shot to shot. If you've already bought the Breville Barista Express and outgrown the integrated grinder, this is the upgrade path.

**Best for**: Dedicated home espresso enthusiasts who want to close the gap between home and specialty shop extraction quality.

## How to Choose a Coffee Grinder

### Burr Grinder vs. Blade Grinder: The Key Difference

A blade grinder chops coffee into irregular particles of different sizes. Large particles under-extract (weak, sour), small particles over-extract (bitter, harsh) — in the same cup, simultaneously. The result is coffee that tastes neither fully extracted nor consistently brewed.

A burr grinder crushes coffee between two abrasive surfaces at a precise distance. Every particle exits at roughly the same size. The extraction is even. The improvement over blade grinding is noticeable on the first cup; it is not subtle.

If you own a blade grinder, replacing it is the single highest-impact upgrade you can make to your coffee setup.

### Conical vs. Flat Burrs

**Conical burrs**: one cone-shaped burr rotating inside another. Lower RPM, less heat generation, slightly more forgiving of inconsistent grind pressure. Standard design for home grinders across all price points.

**Flat burrs**: two parallel disc-shaped burrs. More consistent particle size at coarser settings (filter coffee), can generate more heat at high RPM. Used in most commercial grinders. The Fellow Ode Gen 2 and Niche Zero use flat burr designs.

For home use at most price points, the difference is less significant than burr diameter and quality.

### Grind Settings: How Many Do You Need?

- **Pour-over / drip / French press only**: 15 settings sufficient
- **Multiple brew methods including espresso**: 40+ settings preferred; espresso requires fine-grained adjustment
- **Espresso only or primary**: stepless adjustment preferred; click-stop grinders can't dial between steps

### Retention: Why It Matters

Coffee grounds go stale faster than whole beans. Grinders that retain 2–5g between uses mean your first dose contains yesterday's ground coffee. For low-volume home use (one dose daily), this matters less. For multi-dose households or for espresso dialling-in where you're discarding test shots, low-retention grinders (Niche Zero, Fellow Ode, 1Zpresso) are worth the premium.
